Parking at SJSU

Quick tips, tricks, and options for getting parked near campus. Always verify the latest rules and pricing on official sources.

Arrive Off‑Peak

Spaces are easier before 9am and after 2pm. Budget extra time during midterms/finals and game days.

Know Your Permit

Student permits are often required for university garages. Check eligible lots and hours for your permit type.

Use Apps & Live Info

Many garages and city lots publish live or recent availability. Map apps can surface rates and time limits.

On‑Campus Garages

  • Student permit access during posted hours; event days may vary.
  • Observe signage for levels, EV stalls, and time‑restricted areas.
  • Keep your permit/plate info handy for enforcement checks.

Tip: Arrive with a back‑up lot in mind if your first choice is full.

City & Private Lots

  • Downtown garages often offer evening/weekend rates.
  • Always check posted pricing and time limits before parking.
  • Validate when visiting venues that offer reduced rates.

Note: Rates and terms change frequently; verify at the lot entrance.

Permits

Quarter/semester permits may be available for students. Eligibility, pricing, and zones vary.

Daily/Hourly

Many garages support pay‑per‑day or hourly rates. Keep your license plate number handy for kiosks.

Mobile Payment

Some lots support mobile apps or license‑plate recognition for quick payment and renewals.

EV Charging

Look for clearly signed EV stalls. Fees, max times, and access rules differ by location.

Accessible Parking

Accessible stalls require proper placards/plates. Check campus policy for how permits interact with accessibility designations.

Safety & Security

Park in well‑lit areas, avoid leaving valuables visible, and note emergency call box locations.

Bikes & Scooters

Use designated racks and follow signage. Lock your frame and wheel; store scooters where allowed.

Alternatives to Driving

Transit (VTA/Light Rail)

Use transit‑friendly routes and stops near campus to skip parking altogether.

Micromobility

Bike, scooter, or e‑bike can be faster during peak hours. Lock up at designated racks.

Carpools

Carpooling reduces costs and parking demand; some lots have carpool incentives.

Rideshare

Get dropped off near campus to avoid the search and fees.
