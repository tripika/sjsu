International Students at SJSU

Key things to know before you arrive, and resources to help you settle in and succeed at San Jose State University.

Before You Arrive

Visa & I‑20/DS‑2019

Confirm start date, keep full‑time status, and carry required documents while traveling.

Immunizations & Health

Upload immunizations if required; review student health insurance coverage and clinics.

Finances

Plan for tuition, housing deposits, and first‑month expenses. Consider opening a U.S. bank account after arrival.

Housing

Book temporary stay for your first nights. Explore campus housing and nearby neighborhoods.

Communications

Research U.S. phone plans/SIMs. Save campus contacts and enable international roaming for travel.

Packing & Travel

Carry originals and copies of documents. Check customs rules for electronics/medications.

Your First Week

  • Attend ISSS orientation and any department welcome sessions.
  • Get your student ID and activate campus accounts.
  • Set up banking and a mobile plan; learn local transit routes.
  • Explore campus buildings, libraries, and study spaces.

Work Authorization (Overview)

F‑1 students may qualify for on‑campus work, CPT (Curricular Practical Training), and OPT (Optional Practical Training). J‑1 students may have Academic Training options. Always consult ISSS before accepting employment.

Keep your status by following enrollment requirements and reporting address changes on time.

Everyday Logistics

Banking

Bring passport and school docs to open an account. Ask about student accounts with low fees.

Phone & Data

Prepaid or student plans can be flexible. Ensure your device supports U.S. bands/eSIM.

Healthcare

Know your plan’s clinics and urgent care. Carry insurance card and ID.

Transportation

Learn VTA routes and bike rules. Consider rideshare for late evenings.

Documents & IDs

  • After arrival, verify your I‑94 travel record online.
  • Complete campus check‑in and attend required orientations.
  • Get your student ID and set up two‑factor authentication.
